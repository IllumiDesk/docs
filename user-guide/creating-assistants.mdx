## Create Your First AI Assistant

Follow this guide to build a powerful AI assistant that can help your users with their questions and tasks.

## Prerequisites

Before creating your assistant, make sure you have:

- An active IllumiChat account
- Content ready for your knowledge base (optional but recommended)
- A clear understanding of your assistant's purpose

## Step 1: Access the Assistant Builder

1. Log in to your [IllumiChat dashboard](https://app.illumichat.com)
2. Click **"Create Assistant"** from the main dashboard
3. You'll be taken to the assistant creation wizard

## Step 2: Basic Configuration

Fill in the essential details for your assistant:

### Assistant Information

<ParamField body="name" type="string" required>
The name of your assistant. Choose something descriptive and memorable.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="description" type="string" required>
A brief description of what your assistant does and who it helps.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="category" type="string">
Select the category that best describes your assistant's purpose (e.g., Customer Support, Sales, Internal Knowledge).
</ParamField>

### Personality Settings

Configure how your assistant communicates:

<ParamField body="tone" type="string" default="professional">
Choose the communication tone: professional, friendly, casual, or formal.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="style" type="string" default="helpful">
Define the response style: helpful, direct, detailed, or concise.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="expertise_level" type="string" default="intermediate">
Set the technical expertise level: beginner, intermediate, or advanced.
</ParamField>

<Example>
**Professional Customer Support Assistant**:
- Name: "TechSupport Pro"
- Description: "Expert technical support assistant for software troubleshooting"
- Tone: Professional
- Style: Helpful
- Expertise: Advanced
</Example>

## Step 3: Knowledge Base Setup

Connect your assistant to relevant information:

### Upload Documents

1. Click **"Add Knowledge"** in the assistant builder
2. Choose **"Upload Documents"**
3. Select files from your computer or drag and drop
4. Supported formats: PDF, Word (.docx), Text (.txt), Markdown (.md)

<Tip>
Start with your most important documents first. You can always add more content later.
</Tip>

### Add Web Content

1. Click **"Add Knowledge"** → **"Web Content"**
2. Enter the URL of the website you want to crawl
3. Choose crawling depth (pages to include)
4. Review and approve the content before adding

<Warning>
Only crawl websites you own or have permission to use. Respect robots.txt and rate limits.
</Warning>

### Create Custom Knowledge

Add specific information manually:

1. Click **"Add Knowledge"** → **"Custom Entry"**
2. Choose the type: FAQ, Procedure, Definition, or Example
3. Fill in the title and content
4. Add relevant tags for better organization

## Step 4: Advanced Configuration

### Response Settings

<ParamField body="max_response_length" type="integer" default="500">
Maximum number of words in responses. Range: 100-2000.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="include_sources" type="boolean" default="true">
Whether to include source references in responses.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="conversation_memory" type="integer" default="10">
Number of previous messages to remember in conversations.
</ParamField>

### Behavior Rules

Define what your assistant can and cannot do:

<ParamField body="allowed_actions" type="array">
List of actions your assistant is allowed to perform.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="restricted_topics" type="array">
Topics your assistant should avoid or redirect.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="escalation_triggers" type="array">
Conditions that should trigger escalation to human support.
</ParamField>

## Step 5: Test Your Assistant

Before deploying, thoroughly test your assistant:

### Test Scenarios

1. **Basic Questions**: Test common queries about your content
2. **Edge Cases**: Try unusual or complex questions
3. **Boundary Testing**: Test what your assistant won't do
4. **Conversation Flow**: Test multi-turn conversations

### Refine Responses

Based on testing, you may need to:

- Add more knowledge content
- Adjust personality settings
- Modify behavior rules
- Update response settings

<Tip>
Test with real users if possible. Their questions will often reveal gaps in your knowledge base.
</Tip>

## Step 6: Deploy Your Assistant

Choose how to make your assistant available to users:

### Chat Widget

Embed on your website:

1. Go to **"Deploy"** → **"Chat Widget"**
2. Customize appearance (colors, logo, position)
3. Copy the embed code
4. Add to your website

### API Integration

Use programmatically:

1. Go to **"Deploy"** → **"API"**
2. Get your assistant's unique ID
3. Use the API to send messages and receive responses

### Platform Integrations

Deploy to communication platforms:

- **Slack**: Add to your workspace
- **Discord**: Create a bot
- **Microsoft Teams**: Integrate with your team

## Best Practices

### 1. Start Simple

<Info>
Begin with a focused assistant that does one thing well, then expand over time.
</Info>

### 2. Quality Knowledge

- Upload accurate, up-to-date information
- Organize content logically
- Include examples and use cases
- Regular content updates

### 3. Clear Boundaries

- Define what your assistant can and cannot do
- Set appropriate escalation paths
- Include disclaimers when necessary
- Respect privacy and security

### 4. Continuous Improvement

- Monitor usage and feedback
- Identify common questions and gaps
- Update knowledge base regularly
- Refine personality and behavior

## Common Issues and Solutions

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Assistant gives generic responses">
  - Check that your knowledge base has relevant content
  - Review and improve content quality
  - Adjust response settings for more specificity
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Assistant doesn't understand questions">
  - Add more diverse examples to your knowledge base
  - Include common question variations
  - Test with different phrasings
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Responses are too long/short">
  - Adjust the max_response_length setting
  - Modify the response style (detailed vs concise)
  - Review and edit knowledge content
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
